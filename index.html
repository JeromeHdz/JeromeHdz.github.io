What I read

Some Books I Recommend

C# Programming

C# Programming Yellow Book
- Rob Miles

This ebook is a reference book when it comes to teaching C# classes in many universities and gives the reader the basics of C# language and its concepts (or gives the reader the basics and concepts of C# language). Easy to read and effective, this book will guide you and teach you the basics of C# programming. The perfect introduction in C# world for people who love to learn by reading.

Find it on Amazon

Unity3D & Programming

Unity in Action:
Multiplatform Game Development in C# with Unity 5
- Joseph Hocking

This Unity 5 game development book will help you to avoid unnecessary switching among development tools and put your attention on making great games in the Unity game development platform. It shows you how to write and deploy games on Unity 5 platform by following a few easy and effective steps. You will get the understanding to create graphically driven 2D and 3D game applications. With this guide, you will master all the skills to become a successful game developer in Unity 5. No previous experience in Unity is required to read this book so it can be a really good start for people who want to be a Game Developer. Nevertheless You need to have some knowledge of C# to have the best experience.

Find it on Amazon

Game Design & Unity3D & Programming

Introduction to Game Design, Prototyping, and Development
- Jeremy Gibson Bond

I am currently reading this book so I cannot tell you right now what I think about it but as for the few pages that I have read so far the book seems to be the one you should read if you want to learn about Unity3d game engine and at the same time learn the basics of game design and game prototyping.
More information soon.

Find it on Amazon

Game Design

A Theory of Fun for Game Design
- Raph Koster

Small but effective, A Theory of Fun for Game Design by Raph Koster, is the first book I recommend if you are interested in creating video games. Really easy to read and and filled of humour, you will be challenged to think in new ways and to figure out why games appeal to human beings. This books is like an introduction to the game design world and it will bring you to understand the real essence of the “fun” in a game. It’s novel way by providing visuals to illustrate and combined with a narrative way on the art of designing games and challenges make of this book unique and finally as its title, really fun to read for everybody.

Find it on Amazon

Game Design

The Art of Game Design:
A Book of Lenses
- Jesse Schell

After “A theory of Fun for Game Design”, I strongly recommend “The Art of Game Design”. A must have read for every Game designer, “The Art of Game Design: A Book of Lenses”. You will not only go through the game design process while analyzing each step a designer must take to build their ideas but it also presents over 100 questions to help you think about the psychology, gameplay, usability, storyline, and what makes a game fun. This book will guide you create and see your game become real. Through this different perspective, you will be force to think about how other people would see your game.

Find it on Amazon

Animation & Motion Design

Design for Motion: Fundamentals and Techniques of Motion Design
- Austin Shaw

Interesting by the design and the motion design for a long time, I started by work with Adobe After Effect to create Visual Effects for short movies. Little by little I started to create, for personnal and comemrcial purpose, some motion designs and animated project. But my lack of experience in this field guide me to read this book to learn the basics. In his book, Design for Motion, Austin breaks down the essential techniques and design principles that new motion designers should use in order to craft not only compelling stories, but compelling art.

Find it on Amazon
